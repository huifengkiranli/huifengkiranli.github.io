My baseline view on global macro outlook in 2022

Posted on 2021-11-06 In Economics

Last month, IMF released the World Economic Outlook - October version, showing an optimistic outlook in 2022, which dovetails with my opinion to some extent.

And this chart here just shows the average annual growth rates for each calendar year 2020-2022, and the key point I want to take from this is that, actually if you look at the average growth rate for 2022, it’s only marginally slower than IMF forecast for 2021, that slowdown in the annual growth rate from about 5.9 to 4.9 next year, is mainly down to base effects, caused by the sharp contractions last year, boosting this year’s annual growth rates. So really the underlying picture when you look through those base effects, is another year of incredibly strong growth, certainly by post-global financial crisis standards.

And that reflects three phenomena:

1. Recovery from pandemic lows: So first, for a number of economies, it’s still the case that there’s some easy growth wins from overcoming the pandemic, that is expected in 2022.

2. Economic activities normalcy: The second issue is Omicron, there’s still huge uncertainties around that at the moment, but my view remains that at a global level, over 2022 COVID is going to be less of an economic issue, and much more a health issue than it has been the case up until now, really reflecting the ongoing vaccine progress, as well as other medical developments, which should ensure that there’s much less need for heavy restrictions that curb economic activity in the future relative to the past.

3. Easing supply chain: The final point for optimism, certainly relative to 2021 is really that, I feel the global supply chain pressures and bottlenecks, that have become a huge feature of this year, should begin to ease over the course of 2022, and that should ensure that manufacturing production can start to rise again, having had essentially a year of flatlining in 2021.

So that’s really some of the key reasons why I retain a fairly optimistic view on the pace of growth, in 2022 at a global level. But it’s worth noting that, as we move into 2022, I see the recovery entering a new phase, where the supply chain and supply side issues that have been so important in 2021, determining the pace of growth at a global level, which may dissipate. But at the same time, I’m expecting more demand side factors, to become a lot more important, in terms of determining the exact pace of growth that we see in 2022.

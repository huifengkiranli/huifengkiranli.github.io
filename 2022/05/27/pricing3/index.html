Selling Prices for Target Margin, Discount and Rebate

Posted on 2022-05-27 In Pricing

Fig. 1 Selling prices model

This is a model for calculating selling prices given the cost, target margin, discount and rebate through Excel Solver. As you can see from the result above, even though cost is same for three product, different target discount and rebate results in varied gross profit. Managing cost is important to manage profit, while in this case, how to set target discount and rebate is vital for profit generation. Discount and rebate are commonly used terms in today’s dynamic markets, both are distinct forms of price-cuts that directly or indirectly promote the overall sales of a business. They may sound similar, however, there is a considerable difference between discount and rebate. A good pricing practice should review discount or rebate strategy and start by setting goals at least annually.

1. Difference Between Discount and Rebate

Fig. 2 Discount vs. Rebate

2. Discount

For many companies, in order to push customer to buy a bit more volume of product, so it can reach the targeted volume, the companies tend to provide some discount. While sometimes unstructured discounting does increase sales to some degree, rather increase company profit. It is important to create a structured customer reward mechanism to guide sales people to more profitable contracts by defining different type of discount for different specific purpose.

Fig. 3 Price waterfall with structured discount

Dangers of Volume discounts

  • Forward buying, diverting
  • Larger, more powerful customers take advantage of small customers
  • According confusion about costs, margins, prices

3. Rebate

A company should review rebate strategy at least annually, for each channel, customer, or segment, what behavior are you trying to achieve? For each desired behavior, establish which type of rebate to employ. I think it is good to create a set of rebate templates: a rebate playbook to help cut down complexity while ensuring the right rebate is applied to the right customer objective.
